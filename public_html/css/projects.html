SmartO.N.E. Business Cards & On-line Stakeholder Social Network

SmartO.N.E. (Optimized Networking Expediter) advances the way we network and communicate between diverse stakeholders. Combining the uses of the business card and the rolodex, we take passing and storing contact information to a more convenient, environmentally friendly, cost-efficient, and accessible way. With the combination of the card and customized social network, it encourages, builds, and stimulates community engagement. As well as, creating transparency for individuals to know who’s who amongst local stakeholders.

These “SmartONE card come with access to an on-line, localized community stakeholder profile directory. These stakeholders are leaders and professionals in business, local govt, and residents who choose to opt-in and express interest or promote initiatives. All for the purpose of creating opportunities for partnerships and supporters who would be willing to leverage their resources/contribute/support in support of a mutual goal. In doing so, we raise the social capital for communities and optimize the capacity of positive development and quality of living for everyone.



Methodology

Methodology / Technology This project draws upon my work experiences as a Community Development Associate at Silicon Valley Leadership Group in San Jose, CA. My role was to represent the interest of tech businesses in policy initiatives affecting local communities. Primarily, by identifying and reaching out to local government and community leaders as prospective partners in their plans for positively developing Silicon Valley for their employer base.

It was through this experience that I observed how hindered communications were between these diverse stakeholder groups, despite their equal enthusiasm to work with each other to advance mutual interests. Even with all the advancements in technology and the ease of outreach through social media, stakeholders from these various institutions still felt a prevailing uneasiness to directly contact each other for many reasons.

Currently, the primary way we are building these networks is through the use of business cards. And the way we store them (despite its obvious ‘outdated-ness’) forms the primary use of our social network database when we meet new contacts. This project aims to alleviate these inconveniences and reduce communication barriers by boosting our ability to build constructive social and professional networks with comfort and ease.
